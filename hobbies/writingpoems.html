Introduction

Writing Poetry has not always been something that I would do in my leisure time or at times when I am stressed but it has been a friend since covid times. I agree I don't write like a professional poet would but it helps me deal with life in so many ways. I have written multiple poems many of which were praised by my peers and acquaintances. Writing down what I am feeling in words has helped me in may different ways. It made me more aware of what I really am, what I expect from other people, and how I expect to act myself.A few of the poems I have written are shown below.

  1. "He Is There"

    There she stands in all her glory, hiding her own story.
    Afraid of the lingering eyes, as her breath fall and rise.
    Her smile on display, that suited the role she had to play.
    Her sweet words, that made everyone’s temper to curb.
    Watching her father’s eyes, she swears she would have cried.
    He knew she was faking; he knew she was inside breaking.
    His smile made her feel relieved, “He is there” she believed.

    There she sat with her heart at pain, all her efforts were vain.
    Her heart wrenching sobs were audible, pushing that out of her thoughts seemed impossible.
    She was now her true self, the one even she hated herself.
    She was a weakling, and all her feelings didn’t help her in healing.
    All the words still rung in her mind, why is she the one everyone is behind??
    A hand landed on her shoulder, “I am her for you” the possessor of the hand told her.
    He knew she felt weak, he knew why she will never speak.
    His embrace made her feel secured, “he is there” she was assured.

    There she is trying to fly, wishing to experience everything before she dies.
    Her eyes still void, but she was no more annoyed.
    She still cries, though not after every sun rise.
    Her smile was there, and it wasn’t fake everybody was aware.
    She loved herself, no more worrying about who said “you should change yourself...”
    His hand was in her grip, her true polite words that spill out her lips.
    His hold was her strength, “he is there” made her feel content.

  2. "Angels Aren't Real"

    In the blue sky with the wings open wide
    There is the angel that survived
    Bloodshed, war and tears of sorrow.
    But never turned down her hope narrow.
    She kept this all in her till now
    Because she was angel and having emotions was not allowed.

    There she is now flying so high With a triumph smile held in pride.
    But still, she is incomplete Finding for the soul she feels
    Is around but never seen
    And is far from what is reality.
    A soul who is busy chasing his own dreams
    She felt hurt but never showed
    Because she was an angel and was the one to hold all the load.

    She is still there trying
    With her eyes filled with the little whines.
    The whines that she was never supposed to suppress.
    But she was an angel and can’t object.

    All her hope came tumbling back
    When all her dreams were thrown down the rack
    By the only soul who she wanted to have her back
    Who she thought was always stay
    Was just a false hope of the ray
    But she was an angel
    and staying quite was the only way.

    She took her time to learn it all again
    Because she was an angel and not allowed to show her pain.
    Her lips started curving up again
    Because all her pain just made her gains
    There is she now with her head actually held up high
    Because she is the only one in sky.
    The soul is now an old chapter
    Whose existence is no longer her matter. She is free to laugh and smile
    After travelling the rough road for miles.
    The road that led her here today
    And she can confidently say
    Whatever she feels Whatever is real
    Because she now knows that “Angels are not real”.
